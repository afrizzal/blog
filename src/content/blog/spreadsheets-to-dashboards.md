Every team I've joined had a spreadsheet quietly running part of the business. That's not a
failure — it's a signal of what to build next.

## The spreadsheet is the spec

A well-used sheet tells you exactly which numbers people care about and how they actually
work. Treat it as a free requirements document.

## Promoting it safely

Move the logic into a real datasource, keep the same columns people already trust, and only
then change the interface. Familiarity first, features second.

Done right, nobody mourns the spreadsheet — they just stop emailing each other versions of it.
