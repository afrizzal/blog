AI demos are easy. AI in production that survives Monday morning is not.

## Beyond the demo

A prompt that works on three examples will fail on the fourth in ways a customer notices. The
hard parts are the boring parts: validation, fallbacks, logging, and knowing when to hand off
to a human.

## My rule of thumb

Automate the repetitive 80%, route the messy 20% to a person, and measure both. If you can't
measure it, you can't trust it — and you definitely can't improve it.

Used this way, automation buys back hours without quietly burning trust.
